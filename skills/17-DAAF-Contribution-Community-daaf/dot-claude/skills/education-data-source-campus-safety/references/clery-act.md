# The Clery Act: Legal Framework and Requirements

## History and Background

### Origin

The Jeanne Clery Disclosure of Campus Security Policy and Campus Crime Statistics Act (Clery Act) originated from a tragic incident:

- **April 5, 1986**: Jeanne Clery, a 19-year-old freshman at Lehigh University, was raped and murdered in her residence hall
- **Discovery**: Her parents later learned this was one of 38 violent crimes at Lehigh over three years—information not disclosed to prospective students
- **Legal action**: The Clery family sued Lehigh and won a $2 million settlement
- **Advocacy**: The Clerys lobbied Congress for legislation requiring campus crime disclosure

### Legislative Evolution

| Year | Action | Key Changes |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1990 | Crime Awareness and Campus Security Act | Original law passed |
| 1992 | Campus Sexual Assault Victims' Bill of Rights | Added sexual assault policies |
| 1998 | Renamed to Clery Act | Expanded requirements |
| 2000 | Campus Sex Crimes Prevention Act | Sex offender registry notification |
| 2008 | Higher Education Opportunity Act (HEOA) | Added fire safety, emergency notification |
| 2013 | Violence Against Women Reauthorization Act (VAWA) | Added dating violence, domestic violence, stalking |
| 2022 | VAWA Reauthorization | Updated training requirements |
| 2024 | Stop Campus Hazing Act | Added hazing transparency requirements |

## Applicability

### Covered Institutions

The Clery Act applies to:

- All postsecondary institutions participating in Title IV federal student aid programs
- Public and private institutions
- Two-year and four-year institutions
- Vocational and trade schools receiving federal aid
- Branch campuses (reported separately)

### Exemptions

- Institutions not receiving Title IV funding
- Foreign campuses of U.S. institutions (limited reporting)
- Study abroad programs (limited reporting)

## Core Requirements

### 1. Annual Security Report (ASR)

Institutions must publish and distribute an Annual Security Report by **October 1** each year containing:

**Crime Statistics (3 years)**:
- All Clery-reportable crimes
- By geographic category (on-campus, residence halls, public property, noncampus)
- For each separate campus

**Policy Statements**:
- Campus law enforcement authority and jurisdiction
- Policies for reporting crimes
- Security awareness programs
- Crime prevention programs
- Drug and alcohol policies
- Sexual assault prevention and response procedures
- Missing student notification procedures
- Emergency response and evacuation procedures

### 2. Crime Statistics Reporting

**What must be reported**:
- Crimes reported to Campus Security Authorities (CSAs)
- Crimes reported to local law enforcement
- Crimes occurring within Clery geography

**Who reports**:
- Campus Security Authorities (CSAs) include:
  - Campus police/security departments
  - Individuals with security responsibilities
  - Officials with significant responsibility for student activities
  - Designated officials to whom crimes should be reported

**CSA Categories**:
1. Campus police or security department
2. Non-police security personnel
3. Officials with significant responsibility for student and campus activities
4. Officials designated as those to whom crimes should be reported

### 3. Timely Warnings

Institutions must issue timely warnings when:
- A Clery crime occurs within Clery geography
- The crime poses a serious or ongoing threat to the campus community
- Warning would not compromise law enforcement efforts

**Requirements**:
- Must be issued as soon as pertinent information is available
- Must aid in prevention of similar crimes
- Must be distributed campus-wide

### 4. Emergency Notification

Institutions must:
- Immediately notify the campus community of significant emergencies or dangerous situations
- Have procedures to confirm the emergency
- Determine appropriate notification content
- Initiate notification without delay (unless it would compromise efforts to contain the emergency)

### 5. Daily Crime Log

Campus police/security departments must maintain a daily crime log:

**Required Information**:
- Nature of crime
- Date/time crime was reported
- Date/time crime occurred
- General location
- Disposition of complaint (if known)

**Accessibility**:
- Most recent 60 days: Available for public inspection during business hours
- Older entries: Available within 2 business days of request
- Entries must be made within 2 business days of report

### 6. Fire Safety Requirements (HEOA 2008)

Institutions with on-campus student housing must:

**Annual Fire Safety Report**:
- Fire statistics for each on-campus student housing facility
- Fire safety policies
- Fire safety education programs
- Plans for future fire safety improvements

**Fire Log**:
- Similar to crime log for fire incidents
- Public accessibility requirements

## Campus Security Authorities (CSAs)

### Definition

A CSA is an official with significant responsibility for student and campus activities. The designation is based on function, not title.

### CSA Examples

**Always CSAs**:
- Campus police officers
- Security guards
- Contract security personnel

**Typically CSAs**:
- Dean of Students
- Director of Student Activities
- Director of Athletics
- Athletic coaches
- Faculty advisors to student organizations
- Resident Advisors/Assistants
- Title IX Coordinator
- Ombudsperson

**Generally NOT CSAs**:
- Faculty without additional student responsibility
- Clerical staff
- Physicians in student health (if only treating)
- Counselors (if providing mental health counseling)
- Pastoral counselors

### CSA Reporting Obligations

CSAs must:
- Report crimes reported to them to the institution's Clery compliance office
- Report statistics only (not conduct investigations)
- Report even if victim requests confidentiality (statistics only, not identifying information)

## Enforcement

### Department of Education Oversight

The Clery Act is enforced by the Department of Education's Clery Act Compliance Division within Federal Student Aid.

**Enforcement Actions**:
- Program reviews (desk and on-site)
- Complaint investigations
- Compliance agreements
- Fines up to **$67,544 per violation** (as of 2023, adjusted annually for inflation)
- Suspension or termination of Title IV eligibility

### Notable Enforcement Actions

| Institution | Year | Fine | Issues |
|-------------|------|------|--------|
| Penn State | 2012 | $2.4M | Underreporting, policy failures (Sandusky case) |
| Virginia Tech | 2014 | $55K | Timely warning failures (2007 shooting) |
| Michigan State | 2019 | $4.5M | Underreporting, Nassar case failures |

## Relationship to Other Laws

### Title IX

- **Overlap**: Sexual assault, dating violence, domestic violence, stalking
- **Difference**: Title IX focuses on discrimination; Clery focuses on disclosure
- **Coordination**: Same incidents may trigger both Title IX and Clery obligations

### FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act)

- **Issue**: Balancing crime disclosure with student privacy
- **Resolution**: Clery Act creates exception allowing disclosure of crime information
- **Limitation**: Victim names generally protected unless victim consents

### VAWA (Violence Against Women Act)

- **2013 Amendments**: Significantly expanded Clery requirements
- **Added crimes**: Dating violence, domestic violence, stalking
- **Added policies**: Prevention programs, victim rights, disciplinary procedures

### State Laws

Many states have enacted additional campus safety laws:
- Some require broader crime reporting
- Some mandate specific prevention programs
- Some impose additional notification requirements
- State laws may require reporting crimes not covered by Clery

| Deadline | Requirement |
