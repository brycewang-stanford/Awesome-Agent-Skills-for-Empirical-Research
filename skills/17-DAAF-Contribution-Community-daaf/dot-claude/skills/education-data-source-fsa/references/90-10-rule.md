## Overview

The 90/10 rule is a federal requirement that proprietary (for-profit) institutions of higher education must derive at least 10% of their revenue from sources other than Title IV federal student aid. This rule is designed to ensure market viability and prevent schools from existing solely on federal funds.

### Purpose

The 90/10 rule serves to:

1. **Ensure market validation**: Schools must attract some non-federal investment
2. **Protect taxpayers**: Prevents institutions from being 100% federally funded
3. **Encourage quality**: Assumption that quality schools attract non-federal funds
4. **Provide early warning**: High Title IV percentages may indicate problems

### Applicability

| Institution Type | Subject to 90/10? |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Proprietary (for-profit) | Yes |
| Private nonprofit | No |
| Public institutions | No |

### Regulatory Authority

- Section 487(a)(24) of the Higher Education Act
- 34 CFR 668.28 - Non-Federal revenue (90/10)
- American Rescue Plan Act of 2021 (modified to include military benefits)

## Rule Requirements

### Basic Requirement

Proprietary institutions must derive **at least 10%** of revenue from sources other than Title IV federal student aid.

Stated differently: No more than **90%** of revenue may come from Title IV programs.

### Calculation Period

- Calculated on a fiscal year basis
- Reported annually to the Department of Education
- Based on institutional fiscal year (not award year)

### Current Standard (Post-2023)

Following the American Rescue Plan Act modifications:

| Revenue Source | Counted As |
|----------------|------------|
| Title IV (Pell, Direct Loans, etc.) | Federal (numerator) |
| VA education benefits (GI Bill) | Federal (numerator) |
| DoD Tuition Assistance | Federal (numerator) |
| Other federal education funds | Federal (numerator) |
| Cash payments from students | Non-federal |
| Private loans | Non-federal |
| Employer tuition reimbursement | Non-federal |
| State grants | Non-federal |
| Institutional scholarships | Non-federal |

### Formula

```
Title IV Percentage = (Title IV Revenue / Total Revenue) × 100
```

**Compliance Threshold**: Title IV Percentage ≤ 90%

Certain items are excluded from the calculation entirely:

|-----------|--------|

| Revenue from non-eligible programs | Not Title IV-related |
| Revenue from programs not leading to degree/certificate | Context-dependent |

## Calculation Methodology

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Identify fiscal year**: Institution's fiscal year end
2. **Calculate total revenue**: All revenue from Title IV-eligible programs
3. **Calculate Title IV revenue**: Federal student aid disbursed
4. **Apply formula**: Title IV / Total = Percentage
5. **Compare to threshold**: Must be ≤ 90%

### Example Calculation

| Revenue Source | Amount | Classification |
|----------------|--------|----------------|
| Pell Grants | $5,000,000 | Title IV |
| Direct Loans (net of refunds) | $8,000,000 | Title IV |
| Student cash payments | $1,500,000 | Non-federal |
| Private loans | $500,000 | Non-federal |
| **Total Revenue** | **$15,000,000** | |
| **Title IV Revenue** | **$13,000,000** | |
| **Title IV Percentage** | **86.7%** | Compliant (< 90%) |

### Adjustments and Timing

- Revenue recognized when educational services provided
- Loan disbursements count when funds received
- R2T4 (Return of Title IV) reduces Title IV revenue
- Multi-location institutions: calculated at institutional level

## Compliance and Consequences

### Monitoring

- Institutions self-report 90/10 percentages annually
- Department reviews as part of program review
- Auditors verify calculations
- Data published in FSA Data Center

### Warning Threshold

| Percentage | Status |
|------------|--------|
| ≤ 85% | Comfortable margin |
| 85-89% | Approaching threshold |
| 89-90% | Minimal margin |
| > 90% | Violation |

### Consequences of Violation

**Single Year Violation (> 90%):**
- Institution placed on provisional certification
- Must demonstrate corrective action
- Enhanced monitoring

**Two Consecutive Years (> 90%):**
- Loss of Title IV eligibility
- Students can no longer receive federal aid at institution
- Potential closure or major restructuring

### Provisional Certification

When an institution exceeds 90%:

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Shortened certification | Max 2 years |
| Reporting requirements | More frequent 90/10 reporting |
| Corrective action plan | Must show path to compliance |
| Student notifications | May be required |

## Legislative History

### Original 85/15 Rule (1992)

- Enacted as part of 1992 HEA amendments
- Required 15% non-federal revenue
- Modeled after VA's 85/15 rule

### Conversion to 90/10 (1998)

- Threshold relaxed to 90% Title IV
- Response to industry lobbying
- Aimed to provide more flexibility

### American Rescue Plan Act (2021)

**Key Changes:**
- Expanded definition of "federal" revenue
- VA benefits now count as federal
- DoD Tuition Assistance counts as federal
- Effective for fiscal years beginning after January 1, 2023

**Rationale:**
- Close "loophole" where schools targeted veterans/military
- Ensure true market-based non-federal revenue
- Prevent predatory recruiting of military-connected students

### 2025 Interpretive Rule Changes

Recent guidance has allowed:
- Revenue from online programs not eligible for Title IV may count toward 10%
- Clarifications on distance education revenue treatment
- Changes remain subject to ongoing interpretation

## Important Caveats

1. **Only proprietary institutions**: Rule does not apply to public or nonprofit schools
2. **Data lag**: Published data may be 2-4 years old
3. **Methodology changes**: 2021 changes significantly altered calculations
4. **Self-reported**: Initial data from institutions, subject to audit
5. **Net vs. gross**: Various netting rules affect calculations
6. **Program-level detail**: Institutional totals may mask program variations
7. **Online revenue treatment**: Recent guidance changes may affect comparisons

## Research Considerations

When using 90/10 data:

- **Context**: High percentages may reflect student demographics, not quality
- **Timing**: Check whether pre- or post-2021 methodology applies
- **Completeness**: Not all proprietary institutions may have data
- **Interpretation**: Near-90% is not the same as above-90% (provisional vs. ineligible)
- **Complementary data**: Combine with financial responsibility, outcomes, complaints
