# NACUBO-Commonfund Study of Endowments

Comprehensive reference on the NCSE methodology, history, and participation.

> **Portal Scope Note:** The Education Data Portal mirror contains a limited subset of NCSE data (7 columns, 2012-2022). This file documents the full NCSE study methodology and context, which is valuable for understanding the provenance and limitations of the Portal data.

## Study Overview

The NACUBO-Commonfund Study of Endowments (NCSE) is the most comprehensive annual survey of higher education endowment management in the United States.

### Key Facts

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Founded | 1974 |
| Current Partner | Commonfund Institute |
| Survey Period | September - December |
| Results Published | Mid-February |
| Fiscal Year | July 1 - June 30 |
| Target Population | ~1,500 institutions |
| Typical Respondents | 650-700 institutions |
| Assets Represented | $800B+ (FY24: $873.7B) |

### What NACUBO Is

The National Association of College and University Business Officers (NACUBO):

- Founded 1962
- Nonprofit professional organization
- Represents chief administrative and financial officers
- 1,700+ member institutions
- Headquarters: Washington, DC

## Partnership History

The study has had different partners over its 50+ year history:

| Period | Study Name | Partner |
|--------|------------|---------|
| 1974-2008 | NACUBO Endowment Study (NES) | Various |
| 2009-2017 | NACUBO-Commonfund Study of Endowments | Commonfund |
| 2018-2022 | NACUBO-TIAA Study of Endowments (NTSE) | TIAA |
| 2023-present | NACUBO-Commonfund Study of Endowments | Commonfund |

**Important**: When citing historical data, use the correct study name for the year.

### Methodology Consistency

Core methodology has remained consistent across partnerships:

- Same survey questions for key metrics
- Same size and type categorizations
- Same return calculation methods
- Same fiscal year timing

Minor variations may exist in:
- Additional questions added by partners
- Presentation of results
- Supplementary analyses

## Survey Methodology

### Data Collection Process

1. **Invitation** (September): Survey sent to ~1,500 institutions
2. **Response Window**: September through mid-December
3. **Data Cleaning**: December-January
4. **Analysis**: January
5. **Publication**: Mid-February

### Who Can Participate

Eligible participants:
- U.S. colleges and universities
- Affiliated foundations
- System-level funds
- Related nonprofit organizations with endowments

### What is Collected

The survey collects:

**Financial Data**
- Total endowment market value (end of fiscal year)
- Investment returns (1, 3, 5, 10, 25-year periods)
- Asset allocation percentages
- Annual spending/distributions
- New gifts received
- Investment management fees

**Governance Data**
- Spending policy type
- Investment committee structure
- Use of external managers
- ESG/responsible investing practices
- Student-managed fund information

**Institutional Data**
- Institution type (public/private)
- Full-time equivalent students
- Operating budget
- Endowment per FTE student

### Return Calculation

Investment returns are reported as:
- **Time-weighted rate of return**
- **Net of fees** (external management fees excluded)
- **Includes**: Interest, dividends, realized/unrealized gains/losses
- **Period**: Fiscal year ending June 30

**Note on Private Investments**: Survey instructions specify returns for private equity, venture capital, and marketable alternatives should be reported for the one-year period ending June 30 with no "lagged" periods. However, data is self-reported and some lagging may occur.

## Participation Patterns

### Who Participates

Typical participant profile:
- Larger, well-resourced institutions
- Active endowment management programs
- NACUBO members (though membership not required)
- Institutions seeking benchmarking data

### Non-Participants

Institutions less likely to participate:
- Very small endowments (<$25M)
- Institutions without dedicated investment staff
- Community colleges (many lack significant endowments)
- For-profit institutions

### Participation Trends

| Year | Respondents | Total Assets |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| FY2024 | 658 | $873.7B |
| FY2023 | 688 | $839B |
| FY2022 | 678 | $807B |
| FY2021 | 720 | $821B |
| FY2020 | 774 | $637B |

Participation has declined modestly over recent years but represents the vast majority of endowed assets due to concentration at large institutions.

## Data Products

### Public Tables (Free)

Available at nacubo.org:
- Institution names and market values
- Average/median returns by size and type
- Asset allocation averages
- Spending rate averages
- Historical data back to 2002

### Full Report (Paid)

Includes:
- All public table data
- Detailed breakdowns by multiple dimensions
- Governance and policy data
- Executive summary and analysis
- Additional tables and figures

**Pricing**:
- Participants: Free
- NACUBO Members: $250
- Non-members: $1,500

### Institution-Level Dataset (Paid)

Anonymized dataset available for 2019-2024:
- Individual institution data (coded)
- Returns, allocations, spending rates
- **Note**: Institution identities are hidden

## 50th Anniversary (2024)

The 2023 NCSE marked the study's 50th anniversary. NACUBO published a retrospective white paper documenting key developments:

- Evolution from simple market value reporting to comprehensive analysis
- Growth of alternative investments from near-zero to majority allocation
- Development of modern portfolio theory applications
- Changes in spending policies and governance
- Impact of major market events (1987, 2000, 2008, 2020)

## Survey Questions Categories

### Core Financial Questions

1. Beginning market value
2. Ending market value
3. Investment returns (multiple periods)
4. Asset allocation by category
5. Effective spending rate
6. New gifts to endowment
7. Investment management fees

### Spending Policy Questions

1. Type of spending policy
2. Target spending rate
3. Smoothing mechanism
4. Special appropriations

### Governance Questions

1. Investment committee composition
2. Number of external managers
3. Use of OCIO (outsourced CIO)
4. Frequency of asset allocation review

### ESG Questions

1. ESG policy implementation
2. Negative screening practices
3. Impact investing allocation
4. Climate-related policies

## Related Data Sources

### IPEDS Finance Survey

- **Overlap**: Both report endowment market values
- **IPEDS Advantage**: Mandatory, covers all Title IV institutions
- **NCSE Advantage**: Investment details, voluntary depth

### Commonfund Benchmarks Studies

- Separate from NCSE
- Covers operating funds, not just endowments
- Different participant pool

### Foundation Center Data

- For affiliated foundations
- May have different reporting
- Tax return (990-PF) based
