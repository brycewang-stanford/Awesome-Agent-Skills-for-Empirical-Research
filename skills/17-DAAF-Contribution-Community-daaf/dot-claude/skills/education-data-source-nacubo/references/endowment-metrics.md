# Endowment Metrics

Reference for interpreting NACUBO endowment performance metrics: market values, investment returns, spending rates, and budget support.

> **Portal Scope Note:** The Education Data Portal mirror contains only market value data (`endow_total`, `endow_per_fte`, `endow_chg_mktval`). The investment return, spending rate, budget support, and new gift metrics described in this file are available only through the full NCSE study. This reference provides context for understanding the broader endowment landscape when working with Portal data.

## Market Value

### Definition

**Endowment Market Value**: The fair market value of all endowment assets at fiscal year end (June 30).

Includes:
- True (donor-restricted) endowments
- Term endowments
- Quasi-endowments (board-designated)
- Funds functioning as endowment

### Key Statistics (FY2024)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total assets (all participants) | $873.7 billion |
| Mean market value | ~$1.3 billion |
| Median market value | $243 million |
| Institutions <$100M | ~30% of participants |
| Institutions >$1B | ~20% of participants |

### Distribution Characteristics

The distribution of endowment market values is highly skewed:
- Top 10 institutions hold ~35% of total assets
- Top 132 institutions (>$1B) hold ~84% of total assets
- Bottom 200 institutions hold <2% of total assets

### Market Value Per FTE

Calculated as: Total Market Value / Full-Time Equivalent Students

Useful for comparing endowment resources across different institution sizes.

| Percentile | Approximate Value |
|------------|-------------------|
| 25th | ~$15,000/FTE |
| Median | ~$50,000/FTE |
| 75th | ~$150,000/FTE |
| Top institutions | >$1M/FTE |

## Investment Returns

### Calculation Method

Returns are calculated as:
- **Time-weighted rate of return**
- **Net of fees**: Excludes external investment management fees
- **Includes**: Interest, dividends, realized and unrealized gains/losses

### Return Periods

| Period | Description | Typical Use |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| 1-year | FY return | Recent performance |
| 3-year | Annualized average | Short-term trend |
| 5-year | Annualized average | Medium-term trend |
| 10-year | Annualized average | Long-term benchmark (most cited) |
| 25-year | Annualized average | Full market cycle |

One-year returns show high variability:

| Year | Return | Market Context |
|------|--------|----------------|
| FY21 | 30.6% | Post-COVID recovery |
| FY22 | -8.0% | Rising rates, inflation |
| FY23 | 7.7% | Recovery |

**Recommendation**: Use 10-year returns for performance assessment; single-year returns reflect market conditions more than management skill.

## Spending Rates

### Definitions

**Effective Spending Rate**: Actual endowment withdrawals as a percentage of beginning market value.

Formula: `Annual Withdrawals / Beginning Market Value × 100`

**Target Spending Rate**: Rate specified in spending policy (may differ from effective rate).

### Spending Policies

Common policy types:

| Policy Type | Description | Prevalence |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Moving Average | Rate × average of past N years' values | Most common |
| Percent of Market Value | Fixed % of current or prior year value | Common |
| Hybrid | Weighted combo of methods | Growing |
| Inflation-Adjusted | Prior spending + inflation | Less common |

### Typical Moving Average Formulas

- **3-year average**: Rate × (MV_t-1 + MV_t-2 + MV_t-3) / 3
- **12-quarter average**: Rate × average of past 12 quarterly values
- **5-year average**: Rate × average of past 5 fiscal year-end values

## Budget Support

### Definition

**Budget Support**: Endowment withdrawals as a percentage of institutional operating budget.

Formula: `Annual Withdrawals / Operating Budget × 100`

### Budget Support Statistics (FY24)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average across all participants | 14.0% |
| Previous year (FY23) | 10.9% |

### Budget Support by Size Category

| Size Category | Budget Support % |
|---------------|-----------------|

| $1B to $5B | 18.9% |

**Key Pattern**: Larger endowments contribute a greater share of operating budgets, but even small endowments provide meaningful support.

## Intergenerational Equity

### Concept

Endowments operate on the principle of **intergenerational equity**: preserving real (inflation-adjusted) value so future generations have the same level of support as current generations.

### Target Return Components

For sustainable intergenerational equity, long-term returns should cover:

| Component | Typical Target |
|-----------|---------------|
| Spending rate | 4-5% |
| Inflation (HEPI) | 2-3% |
| Management costs | 0.5-1% |
| Growth increment | 0-1% |
| **Total Target** | **7-10%** |

HEPI = Higher Education Price Index (often exceeds CPI)

### Underwater Endowments

An endowment is "underwater" when market value falls below the original gift value. Legal and policy restrictions may limit spending from underwater funds.

## Benchmarking Considerations

### Appropriate Comparisons

Compare institutions by:
- Size category (most important)
- Institution type (public/private)
- Similar asset allocation
- Geographic region (for operating costs)

### Inappropriate Comparisons

Avoid comparing:
- Small endowments to mega-endowments
- Public to private without adjustment
- Different fiscal years
- Different time periods

### Policy Index Comparison

Many institutions compare returns to a custom policy index reflecting their target asset allocation, rather than to other institutions.

Common benchmark: 60/40 stock/bond portfolio

| Period | NCSE Average | 60/40 Benchmark |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| 3-year | 3.4% | ~3.0% |
| 5-year | 8.3% | ~7.5% |
| 10-year | 6.8% | ~6.5% |

Endowments have generally outperformed 60/40 due to alternative investments.
