# Research Institution Building Workflow

This skill guides the creation and growth of research centers focused on economics and public policy.  It is especially relevant for individuals like you who lead university institutes (e.g. the Hibbs Institute) and aim to expand their scope and influence.

## Stage 1 — Vision and Mission Definition

Clarify:
- The long‑term mission (e.g. improve regional economic resilience, advance childcare policy research).
- Core values and principles (e.g. evidence‑based analysis, community engagement).
- Strategic goals (e.g. become a hub for East Texas economic intelligence, influence national policy debates).

Engage key stakeholders (faculty, community leaders, funders) to refine the vision and ensure relevance.

## Stage 2 — Program and Research Agenda

Identify priority research themes that align with the mission, such as:
- Childcare economics and maternal labor supply.
- Regional labor markets and industrial clusters.
- Impacts of AI on work and regional development.
- Water and environmental economics.

Design specific projects, data initiatives, and policy engagement activities under each theme.  Ensure that programs mix long‑term foundational research with timely policy analysis.

## Stage 3 — Organizational Structure and Governance

Decide on:
- Leadership roles (director, deputy directors, advisory boards).
- Staffing needs (research fellows, data scientists, policy analysts, communications specialists).
- Governance processes for decision‑making, budgeting and accountability.

Consider establishing a faculty advisory board and external advisory council.

## Stage 4 — Funding and Partnerships

Develop a diversified funding strategy:
- Identify federal grants, foundation support, corporate sponsorship and philanthropic donors.
- Prepare pitches and proposals that align with funder priorities.
- Establish partnerships with government agencies, non‑profits and industry to co‑develop projects.

Create a development plan with fundraising targets and timelines.

## Stage 5 — Infrastructure and Data Systems

Ensure necessary infrastructure:
- Secure physical space and administrative support.
- Build data systems and repositories (see the data integration skill) to collect, store and share economic data.
- Implement data governance policies to maintain privacy and ethical standards.

Invest in technology and tools for collaboration (e.g. dashboards, research management platforms).

## Stage 6 — Outreach and Impact

Plan engagement strategies:
- Host seminars, conferences and community forums.
- Produce public reports, policy briefs and interactive dashboards.
- Engage media through op‑eds, blogs and podcasts.
- Develop training programs and student fellowships.

Measure impact through metrics such as research citations, policy changes influenced, and community feedback.

## Conclusion

Building a research institution requires vision, strategy and sustained commitment.  This workflow provides a roadmap for defining a mission, designing programs, establishing governance, securing resources and amplifying impact.  It can help you transform your institute into a leading voice in economic research and policy.
