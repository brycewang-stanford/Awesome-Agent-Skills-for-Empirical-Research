# Policy Brief Writing Workflow

This skill guides the creation of policy briefs that synthesize research findings and offer clear recommendations.  It is particularly useful when translating economic research on childcare, labor markets, regional development or AI adoption into guidance for government officials, nonprofit leaders or business stakeholders.

## Stage 1 — Clarify Purpose and Audience

### Define the Objective
Ask the user:
- What decision or action should this policy brief inform?
- Who will read the brief (e.g. city council members, university administrators, community groups)?
- What constraints (length, tone, deadline) should be observed?

### Gather Evidence
Compile relevant research findings, data analysis results and case studies.  Identify key statistics, charts or anecdotes that will support the argument.  Ensure that sources are credible and up to date.

## Stage 2 — Structure and Messaging

### Outline the Brief
Propose a structure such as:
1. **Executive Summary:** A one‑paragraph overview of the issue, evidence and recommendations.
2. **Problem Statement:** Define the challenge (e.g. childcare shortages in East Texas) and its significance.
3. **Evidence and Analysis:** Summarize the economic research, data findings and lessons from other jurisdictions.
4. **Policy Options:** Present alternative solutions, noting benefits and drawbacks.
5. **Recommendations:** Clearly state the preferred course of action, with rationale.
6. **Implementation Considerations:** Address cost, timeline, stakeholders and potential barriers.
7. **Conclusion:** Reinforce the importance of taking action and suggest next steps.

Confirm the outline with the user.

### Message Framing
Discuss how to frame the issue for the audience.  For example:
- Emphasize economic development and workforce benefits when speaking to business leaders.
- Highlight equity and access when addressing community advocates.
Craft a compelling narrative that connects data to human experiences.

## Stage 3 — Drafting and Refinement

### Draft Sections
Write each section using clear, jargon‑free language.  Use bullet points, headings and graphics to improve readability.  Provide citations or footnotes for data and quotes.

### Visual Aids
Identify charts, maps or infographics that can illustrate key points (e.g. regional childcare affordability index, labor market trends).  Ensure that visuals are accessible and include alt‑text if the brief will be used with AI tools.

### Review and Iterate
Collaborate with the user to edit for clarity and persuasiveness.  Ensure that recommendations follow logically from the evidence.  Keep the brief within the desired length (typically 2–4 pages).

## Stage 4 — Quality Assurance and Delivery

Perform a final check for:
- Consistency and accuracy of data and references.
- Tone and style appropriate for the audience.
- Visual clarity and layout.

Help the user prepare an executive summary or talking points.  Suggest ways to disseminate the brief (e.g. presentations, blog posts, stakeholder meetings).

## Conclusion

By following this workflow, you can create policy briefs that effectively translate complex economic research into actionable guidance.  The assistant helps ensure that the document is clear, persuasive and targeted to the right audience.
