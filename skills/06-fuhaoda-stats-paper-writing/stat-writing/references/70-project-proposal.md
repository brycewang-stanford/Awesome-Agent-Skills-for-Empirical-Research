# Research project / proposal guidance

## Full cycle (high level)
A common cycle:
1. Choose a topic.
2. Plan and organize a timeline.
3. Survey existing work and define the gap.
4. Explore the data.
5. Choose methods.
6. Run analyses/simulations and iterate.
7. Write, revise, and respond to feedback.

## Proposal contents (typical)
A proposal usually contains:
- Title + short motivation
- Background / related work (what’s known)
- Problem statement and research questions
- Proposed contribution(s)
- Data description (or data acquisition plan)
- Methods plan (including assumptions and risks)
- Evaluation plan (simulation/application)
- Timeline / milestones
- Risks + contingency plans
- References

## Writing tips
- Be specific about deliverables.
- Identify what could fail (data access, identifiability, computation) and how you will handle it.
